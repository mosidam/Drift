# DRIFT Sauna Hat Launch Plan

## Positioning

DRIFT starts with one flagship SKU: the Sauna Hat. The brand story is not "buy wool." It is the post-training downshift ritual: run hard, breathe down, close the day with controlled heat, then log the ritual in DRIFT.

The app is retention and cross-sell infrastructure. The landing page sells the hat in one clean path.

## First 100 Units

- Primary SKU: DRIFT Sauna Hat.
- Secondary offer: Sauna Downshift Kit only as an upsell.
- Goal: 100 sold hats before pushing oils, running caps, or a broader catalog.
- Proof loop: founding customers log the Sauna Downshift and feed Batch 002 decisions.

## First 10 Reels

1. POV: Sunday sauna routine after a long run.
2. Why I wear a wool hat in a heat room.
3. Post-run downshift: shoes off, water, sauna hat, quiet reset.
4. What is in my sauna bag.

6. Sauna hat as the recovery uniform.
7. One minute before the sauna, one minute after.
8. Trail run to heat room.
9. What DRIFT logs after a sauna session.
10. First 100 sessions: help shape Batch 002.

## 12-Week Operating Rhythm

- Weeks 1-2: product photos, landing page, ten short videos.
- Weeks 3-6: publish three to five videos weekly and read saves, DMs, visits, and email capture.
- Weeks 7-8: gift product to 20 to 30 sauna, biohacking, trail, and hybrid training creators.
- Weeks 9-12: use paid retargeting only if organic signal exists.

## Paid Signal

Do not spend into cold acquisition until at least one organic asset shows real pull: a high-view reel, repeated buyer DMs, or a meaningful visit-to-email rate. Start with retargeting, not broad cold ads.

## App Role

The app should support customers after the first purchase:

- Sauna Downshift logs.
- Ritual streaks.
- Product-linked programs.
- One contextual recommendation, never a noisy catalog.
- Later: lifecycle email based on ritual logs and product ownership.
